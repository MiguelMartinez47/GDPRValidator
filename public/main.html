GDPRValidator web-app

In 2016, the European Union (EU) adopted the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). This new Data Protection Directive replaces the 1995 Data Protection Directive adopted when the Internet was relatively young. Due to the development of technologies based on distributed storage and processing, such as the cloud computing, it has become more difficult to control, monitor, and safeguard the personal data handled by these types of infrastructure, making data breaches more likely.

GDPRValidator i a tool that aims to assist companies that offer cloud services to be GDPR compliant when they manage and store employees’ or customers’ data in the cloud. This tool targets small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) that have migrated their services or part of them to the cloud to take advantage of this technology and be more competitive.

GDPRValidator is a free tool that pretends to help SMEs to assess their GDPR compliance. This main purpose is to make compliance with GDPR easier for data controllers, which provide services by using cloud computing and help the DPOs simplify their work.

It has been designed as a useful resource for any company or professional. It consists of very specific questions displayed over only two checklist pages, which allows users to assess their situation with regard to personal data processing activities carried out by them; that is, to assess whether their systems are adapted to the requirements to use GDPRValidator or if it must perform a risk analysis.

The tool generates different document adapted to a specific company, informative clauses that must be included in any personal data collection forms, contractual data to be annexed to the data controller contract, the records of processing activities and an annex including guidelines for security measures deemed as minimum. These documents do not guarantee a complete GDPR compliance, but they can help the company better understand the regulation and improve its data management strategies.

GDPRValidator is designed for companies that process low-risk personal data, such as, for example, personal data of customers, providers or human resources.

GDPRValidator should not be used in any processing activities involving a high risk for rights and freedoms of persons, such as healthcare data or mass data processing, among others. Although a data protection impact assesment is provided to be coompleted for such cases.

It must be considered that GDPRValidator is a supporting tool, and, therefore, its resulting documents must be adapted and updated to the situation of any processing activities carried out by your organisation. Obtaining said resulting documents does not automatically entail compliance with the GDPR. Therefore this tool nor the UCLM institution or delegate organizatios are responsible for any fault processing carried out after using this tool.

The GDPRValidator tool does not store the information requested or any other. The enquired information is only used for adapting the documentation to the company which is completing the forms.
